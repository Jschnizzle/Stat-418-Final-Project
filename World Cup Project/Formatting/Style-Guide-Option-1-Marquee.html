The Marquee

A restrained, institutional identity that leans Goldman Sachs — deep navy and sky blue on generous white, with UCLA gold reserved as a single decisive accent.

Blend & intent

Roughly 70% Goldman Sachs, 30% UCLA.

This option borrows the Goldman aesthetic: subdued sky blue, a dominant navy, lots of breathing room, and serif headlines that read as established and quietly confident. UCLA enters through its blue lineage and a thin band of gold — used like a ribbon, never a wash. Best if you want the work to feel like a polished institutional report.

Contrast: Navy on white and white on Navy both pass WCAG AA for body text. Gold (#FFD100) is a fill/rule color only — never set text in gold; it fails contrast on white.

Typography

Serif headlines over a clean sans body — the Goldman pairing, built from free fonts so it ports cleanly to LaTeX, R, and PowerPoint.

Display / Headings — Source Serif 4

From data to theory, with judgement.

Body — Source Sans 3

All models are wrong, but some are useful. This option delegates aggressively to a serif voice for titles and a humanist sans for running text, keeping long passages comfortable at small sizes while titles stay authoritative.
